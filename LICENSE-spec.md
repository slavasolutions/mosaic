## Why CC BY 4.0 and not CC0?

The earlier v0.1–v0.5 spec text was released under CC0 1.0 (public
domain dedication). Starting with v0.9, the spec moves to CC BY 4.0
because:

1. Mosaic is a format; the spec is the canonical record of what the
   format means. Attribution preserves provenance: a reader can always
   trace a derivative back to the source spec and check what changed.
2. CC0 cannot be revoked, but new versions of the spec can be released
   under stricter terms. Prior CC0 releases (v0.1–v0.5) remain CC0
   forever; from v0.9 forward, CC BY 4.0 applies.
3. The trademark "Mosaic" (as applied to this format and any
   conformance program) is **not** licensed under CC BY 4.0. See
   `TRADEMARK.md`.
