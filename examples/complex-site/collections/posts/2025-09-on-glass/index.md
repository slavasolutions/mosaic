The shop has a single tall window that runs nearly the full height of the south wall. In the afternoons it throws a slab of light across the proof press that, if you stand in the wrong place, makes it impossible to read a galley. We've cursed it for six years.

But it's also the reason the studio exists at this address. When we first walked through, the realtor was apologizing for the window — said the previous tenant had complained constantly about heat. Iris said "it's a glasshouse" and that was that. We signed the lease a week later.

## What the name doesn't mean

It's not a reference to the song, the McKellen film, or the 1959 Philip Johnson house in Connecticut. We get asked. The truthful version is just: there's a big window, the room gets hot, and a name stuck.

## What it does mean, sort of

A glasshouse is a place you grow things slowly under careful conditions. You can see in from outside, which keeps you honest. And the panes break if you're careless. All three felt apt for a press.
