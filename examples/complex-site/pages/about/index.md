# About Glasshouse

Glasshouse Press was started in 2019 in a converted garage off Belmont, with one Vandercook proof press, two California job cases, and a stack of Mohawk Superfine someone had abandoned. We've added a Heidelberg windmill since, plus a long-arm stitcher and a guillotine that genuinely scares us.

## How we work

We accept one to three new titles per year. A typical project runs four to six months from manuscript to bound copy: design and typesetting take a month, makeready and printing two or three, binding and trim the rest. We don't take rush work and we don't print things we can't proofread.

## Who we work with

Mostly poets and small university presses. Occasionally a museum or an artist who wants something the digital print shops can't do. We turn down more than we accept, usually because of paper.

## What we don't do

We don't do business cards, wedding suites, or production runs over 500. Not because there's anything wrong with those — we're just slow, and we'd rather be slow at one thing.
