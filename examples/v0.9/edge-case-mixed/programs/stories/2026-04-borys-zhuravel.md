When I left Mariupol in March I had two notebooks, a camera, and
the keys to a building that no longer exists. For a year I could
not paint. The work I have done in Lyon since is from memory: the
courtyard outside my old studio, the same view at three times of
day, the same view in winter.

I am not interested in painting the war. I am interested in
painting what I remember of before it.
